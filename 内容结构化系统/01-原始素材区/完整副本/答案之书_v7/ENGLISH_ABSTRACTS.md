## CH01: Meta-Abilities — Learning How to Learn

**Core Question**: What does 125 years of cognitive science say about how humans actually learn?

**Key Sources**: Brown/Roediger/McDaniel (Make It Stick, 2014), Ericsson (Peak, 2016), Csikszentmihalyi (Flow), Newport (Deep Work, 2016), Castelo et al. (2025 RCT on mobile internet)

**Framework**: Retrieval Practice > Rereading (3-4x more effective) → Spaced Repetition (200-300% better long-term retention) → Interleaving (+40% final test accuracy) → Deliberate Practice (not 10,000 hours — focused, feedback-driven, out-of-comfort-zone) → Deep Work (4 modes: Monastic/Bimodal/Rhythmic/Journalistic) → Attention Economics (attention residue: 23min recovery per interruption; AMS addictive scaffolds)

**One Action**: Do one 90-minute deep work block today. Phone in another room. Single task only.

**Core Question**: How do you build psychological antifragility using the best frameworks from clinical psychology?

**Key Sources**: Adler (Individual Psychology, 9-pillar model via StatPearls 2024), Frankl (Logotherapy: dereflection/paradoxical intention/Socratic dialogue), ACT (Hayes), Stoicism (Epictetus/Marcus Aurelius), Taleb (Antifragile, 2012)

**Framework**: Adler's Nine Pillars → Frankl's Meaning Therapy → ACT's Six Deliberate Discomfort Exercises → Stoic Operating System (Dichotomy of Control + Negative Visualization) → Taleb's Antifragility (stressor → growth; fragile/robust/antifragile triad)

**One Action**: Do one "negative visualization" exercise: imagine losing the thing you most take for granted — then write down what you'd still have.

**Core Question**: What are the most transferable mental models from physics, biology, economics, and systems science?

**Key Sources**: Meadows (Thinking in Systems, 2008 — 12 Leverage Points), Tetlock & Mellers (Superforecasting, 2015), Marks (The Most Important Thing, 2011), Dalio (Principles, 2017), Parrish (Farnam Street — Second-Order Thinking)

**Framework**: 9-Model Decision Matrix (Entropy/Evolution/Compound Interest/System1&2/Leverage Points/Pareto/Occam/Stocks&Flows/Feedback Loops) → Bayesian Updating (Tetlock: update frequency = strongest predictor of accuracy; 2.77 vs 1.47 updates per question) → Second-Order Thinking (Marks/Dalio: ask "And then what?"; first-order and second-order consequences often point opposite directions) → Combined OS: pre-decision second-order → during Bayesian → post-decision Bayesian update → review second-order

**One Action**: For your next important decision, draw a "time-unfolded map": consequences at 10 minutes / 10 days / 10 months.
