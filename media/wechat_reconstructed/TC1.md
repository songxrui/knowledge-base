# TC1｜Health is upstream of everything — 5 repair points that form a positive flywheel

1/12
At 22, I stumbled onto something that made me rethink everything I thought I knew about self-improvement:

Your dopamine system isn't a "pleasure center." It's a motivation engine. And I'd been running mine into the ground for 10 years.

2/12
Here's the mechanism (from Anna Lembke's "Dopamine Nation"):

Every time you game/scroll/trade compulsively, dopamine spikes to 2-3x baseline. Then it crashes BELOW baseline. Peak higher = crash deeper. Do this for 10 years = your baseline stays depressed long-term.

3/12
My Notion data over 30 days:

Days with >30min exercise → average motivation 7.2/10
Days with >2h screen entertainment → average motivation 3.1/10

That's not "laziness." That's a hijacked dopamine system making normal life feel unrewarding.

4/12
Fix #1: Cold water exposure. 30 seconds of cold water to the face upon waking. Dopamine increases ~2.5x, sustained 2-3 hours [Sramek et al. 2000, Eur J Appl Physiol; 14C immersion -> plasma dopamine +250% sustained]. Cost: $0. ROI: infinite.

Replaces the "anticipation circuit" — instead of craving evening gaming, you crave morning cold exposure.

5/12
Fix #2: Morning sunlight. 10 minutes of natural light within 15min of waking. No sunglasses, no glass windows (they filter blue wavelengths).

My 14-day experiment data: sleep onset moved from 1:30am → 11:30pm automatically. Not through "trying harder" — through circadian entrainment.

6/12
Fix #3: Fixed wake time > "trying to sleep early."

You can't control when you get sleepy (adenosine accumulation is involuntary). But you CAN control when you wake up. Fixed 7:00am wake → 16 hours later → natural sleep pressure → sleep time auto-adjusts. Use biology, not willpower.

7/12
Fix #4: Diet as experiment, not belief system.

I had 358 dietary notes from 4 books and 0 experiments. Knowledge:Experiment = 358:0. Now: 14-day refined sugar <15g test. 10-hour eating window. Protein ≥1.6g/kg bodyweight. Your body is your lab — not some guru's advice.

8/12
Fix #5: Environment design > willpower.

Atomic Habits 4 Laws reversed: make bad habits invisible + difficult (delete game icons from desktop, phone in another room at night). Make good habits obvious + easy (writing tool front and center, gym clothes laid out night before).

9/12
The counter-consensus: addiction isn't a moral failure. It's a neurobiological state where your dopamine receptors have downregulated so much that normal rewards (reading, walking, deep work) provide zero signal.

You're not weak. You're dopamine-bankrupt. Fix the engine, not the driver.

10/12
All 5 fixes are interconnected. Morning light regulates cortisol → cortisol regulates dopamine → dopamine enables discipline → discipline enables exercise and diet adherence → all of them together create the positive flywheel.

You can't fix one without the others. This is why "just quit gaming" never works.

11/12
What I'd tell my 21-year-old self:

You're not behind. You're not broken. Your dopamine system is just running on fumes after 10 years of hijacking. 30 days of the 5 fixes above and you'll feel like a different person. Not because you "became disciplined" — because your brain chemistry got recalibrated.

12/12
The upstream variable of everything: health.

Career. Relationships. Creativity. Decision quality. All downstream of dopamine baseline, sleep quality, and metabolic flexibility. Fix the engine, and the car drives itself. Ignore the engine, and no amount of "productivity hacks" will save you.

Start tomorrow: 7am wake + 10min sunlight + 30s cold water. One day. Then another.
